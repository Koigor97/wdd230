Site-Purpose Audience Design

Target Audience

The target audience would be members of the community looking to support local businesses, local business owners and entrepreneurs looking start or grow their businesses, local government officials and community leaders looking for information about Chamber’s advocacy and lobbying efforts, business students and individuals looking for resources and information on starting a business. To see how this works, let take two scenarios for example:

Persona 1 – Sarah:
Sarah is a 35-year-old stay-at-home mother with a college and no prior experience in business. She and her husband John have a combined income of R1,250,000 ($75,000) and have three young children. Sarah lives in a small city and is interested in starting her own business. She finds interest in Lynnwood Business Chamber services and would be accessing the website on her smartphone while she’s at home during her free time. Sarah would be looking at the services offer by Lynnwood Business Chamber, resources that can help her start her own business, and learn about upcoming events.

Persona 2 – Koigor:
Koigor is 55-year-old retiree with a high school diploma and 25 years of experience in the retail industry. He has a pension income of R690,889.60 ($40,000) and is married with no children. Koigor lives in a suburban city and have recently started his own business. He has an interest in the Lynnwood Business Chamber services and will be accessing the website on his laptop

Persona 3 - Karen:
Karen a 50-year-old marketing consultant with a graduate degree and over 20 years of experience in the field. She has a household income of $150,000 and is married with two grown children. Scenario: Karen recently moved to a new city and is looking to expand her consulting business. She is interested in networking with other business owners and professionals in the community, as well as learning about upcoming events and resources that can help her grow her business. She will be accessing the Chamber of Commerce website on her laptop during business hours while she's at her office.

Other scenarios may include:

  1. A new resident of the community searching for information on local events and activities offered by the Chamber of Commerce
  2. A job seeker searching for job fairs, networking events or other career development opportunities
  3. A community leader or volunteer looking for information on how to get involved in the Chamber's events and activities
  4. A visitor to the area searching for information on local businesses, restaurants and attractions
  5. A student researching on the local economy and the role of chamber of commerce in it.
